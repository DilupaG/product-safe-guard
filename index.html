Domain

We investigate the complex domain of crime and suicide.

Literature Survey

Machine learning plays a crucial role in suicide prediction and crime forecasting to address pressing issues in Sri Lanka. Researchers have evolved from traditional models to cutting-edge techniques like deep learning and network analysis to enhance prediction accuracy, using extensive datasets to identify patterns and high-risk individuals. This technology not only forecasts suicide trends but also helps allocate healthcare resources more effectively, potentially saving lives. In Sri Lanka, with one of the world's highest suicide rates, the lack of systematic procedures necessitates machine learning analysis, especially focusing on education and occupation levels. This research aims to provide targeted interventions to reduce suicide rates.

On the other hand, high crime rates in Sri Lanka hinder development, and a machine learning-based crime prediction system is essential to enhance public safety, economy, and overall progress. This system forecasts future crimes and identifies emerging crime hotspots, empowering law enforcement and policymakers to allocate resources strategically and address root causes. While machine learning has made progress in crime prediction, this research aims to refine accuracy and dynamically identify hotspots specific to Sri Lanka, ultimately creating a safer and more prosperous nation.

Research Gap

Suicide is a significant public health concern, requiring precise identification of risk factors and early warning signs across various age groups. Two research gaps need attention: developing advanced predictive models that incorporate a wide range of variables, such as age group, reason for suicide, and method used, and understanding the intricate interplay between age groups, education level, and occupation in influencing suicide risk. Another research gap is understanding the intricate relationship between age groups, education level, and occupation in influencing suicide risk. By addressing these two research gaps, comprehensive models can be developed to enhance the effectiveness of suicide prevention strategies and reduce the incidence of suicide in Sri Lanka among different socio-economic groups. The Weighted Random Forest Regressor is a new approach to regression modeling, enhancing accuracy by assigning varying weights to different features within a dataset. This innovative method optimizes feature selection and weighting, prioritizing features with a strong correlation with the target label.

In crime prediction, separate machine learning models for each crime type are proposed to enhance prediction accuracy. An automated method is introduced to determine the best parameters for each ARIMA model, streamlining the parameter selection process and allowing for a deeper understanding of factors influencing different crime types. The research component focuses on advancing accuracy through customization of ARIMA models and dynamic hotspot identification techniques, aiming to significantly improve prediction accuracy. The goal is to equip law enforcement agencies with the necessary tools to proactively combat crime challenges, particularly at the regional level.

Research Problem

The escalating suicide rates in Sri Lanka, primarily due to the COVID-19 pandemic and economic and political instability, are a pressing research issue. The country's suicide classification is limited, focusing on demographic, social, and psychological factors. A research project aims to develop a predictive model for suicide, analyzing historical data and identifying risk factors. This will help healthcare professionals and community organizations identify high-risk individuals and implement targeted interventions. Sri Lanka also faces a growing crime problem, with a lack of proactive crime prevention strategies. The current system is reactive, underutilizing modern technologies and predictive methods. To address this, a comprehensive crime prediction system is proposed, leveraging advanced machine learning techniques. This system aims to enhance prediction accuracy and identify dynamic hotspots, areas with potential future increases in criminal activity.

The research focuses on improving prediction accuracy through cutting-edge machine learning technology and identifying dynamic hotspots, contributing to early intervention and long-term community safety. The development of a robust crime prediction system in Sri Lanka, focusing on dynamic hotspot accuracy improvement using custom ARIMA models and hotspot identification, holds great potential for enhancing community safety and security.

Methodology

The methodology involves a dual approach first, analyzing historical suicide data from Sri Lanka to create advanced predictive models incorporating various factors like age, reasons, methods, education, and occupation. This comprehensive modeling aims to understand the interplay of these factors in suicide risk. Second, introducing the Weighted Random Forest Regressor for enhanced regression modeling by assigning varying weights to features within datasets, prioritizing those highly correlated with the target label. The combined approach advances suicide prevention and regression modeling, with the goal of reducing suicide rates in Sri Lanka and improving predictive accuracy in diverse domains. The user-friendly system interface includes graphical data representations for healthcare professionals and policymakers.

We collected crime-related data from the Sri Lanka Police Department. Our methodology involved implementing separate naive and ARIMA models for each crime type, with the enhancement of the existing ARIMA algorithm using a novel method to determine the best parameters (p, d, q) for each model. We then constructed an ARIMA Ensemble Model to achieve precise crime predictions. Additionally, we implemented Dynamic Hotspot Identification to address evolving crime patterns and devised a data-driven strategy aimed at fostering a safer community. Our approach aimed to bridge the gap between data analysis and effective crime prevention, ensuring a comprehensive and robust methodology.
